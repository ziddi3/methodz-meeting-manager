# Methodz Meeting Manager v1.1 Test Checklist

## Automated Checks

GitHub Actions should complete both jobs:

```text
validate-static-app
browser-smoke-tests
```

The static job must confirm:

- every JavaScript file passes `node --check`
- both HTML entry points exist
- v1.0 and v1.1 configuration and migration modules exist
- v1.1 retention, redaction, archive, and CSS assets are wired
- the service-worker cache name is `methodz-meeting-manager-v1.1.0`
- the manifest remains valid JSON

The browser job runs both the historical smoke suite and `tests/v11-smoke.spec.js`.

## Direct-File Regression

1. Download or clone the repository.
2. Open `meeting.html` directly with a browser.
3. Confirm the meeting form loads without a server.
4. Confirm Retention & Legal Hold appears after Record Roles & Policy.
5. Confirm Retention Review Dashboard and Partner-Safe Export appear before Saved Meeting Records.
6. Save, edit, archive, restore, print, and download an ordinary meeting.
7. Confirm the service-worker status explains that hosted offline caching is unavailable in direct-file mode.

Expected result: the complete meeting workflow remains usable. SHA-256 may fall back to the explicitly labeled compatibility checksum in direct-file mode.

## Retention Defaults

1. Start a new meeting.
2. Confirm the default retention policy is Business Record Review - 7 Years.
3. Confirm a review date is generated from the meeting date.
4. Change the meeting date and choose a different preset with an empty review date.
5. Confirm a review date is generated for year-based presets.
6. Select Permanent / Do Not Dispose.
7. Confirm the review date is cleared.
8. Select Custom Review Date and enter a date manually.

Expected result: presets assist data entry without preventing custom review dates.

## Retention Save and Restore

1. Enter a meeting title and required meeting fields.
2. Select a retention policy, review date, lifecycle status, and note.
3. Save the record.
4. Open the record for editing.
5. Confirm every retention field is restored.
6. Refresh the page and reopen the record.
7. Confirm the values remain stable.
8. Export the record as JSON and inspect `retentionMetadata`.

Expected result: retention metadata survives save, reload, editing, and export.

## Place a Legal Hold

1. Open a saved record for editing.
2. Check Legal hold / preservation hold is active.
3. Enter the authorized person and reason.
4. Save the record.
5. Inspect the saved JSON.

Expected fields:

```text
legalHold.active = true
legalHold.placedBy
legalHold.placedAt
legalHold.reason
holdHistory[0].action = placed
```

6. Save again without changing the hold state.
7. Confirm a duplicate `placed` history entry is not created.

## Release a Legal Hold

1. Open a record with an active hold.
2. Uncheck the hold control.
3. Enter the releasing person and release reason.
4. Save the record.
5. Inspect the saved JSON.

Expected fields:

```text
legalHold.active = false
legalHold.releasedBy
legalHold.releasedAt
legalHold.releaseNote
holdHistory contains placed and released entries
```

## Archive Hold Protection

1. Save a record with an active legal hold.
2. Archive the record.
3. Find it in the Archive Vault.
4. Confirm a Legal Hold badge appears.
5. Confirm Permanent Delete is disabled.
6. Try activating the control with keyboard navigation.

Expected result: permanent deletion remains blocked.

7. Restore the record.
8. Release the hold and save.
9. Archive it again.
10. Confirm Permanent Delete becomes available after the archived copy contains the released hold state.
11. Confirm the normal permanent-delete confirmation is still required.

## Retention Dashboard

Create records covering these cases:

- future review date
- past review date
- no review date
- permanent policy
- active legal hold
- archived active hold

Confirm dashboard totals for:

- records
- legal holds
- review due
- missing dates

Test every filter and search by title, meeting number, and organization.

Export the report and confirm it contains only retention index data, not complete meeting notes or signatures.

## Partner-Safe Export

1. Create a record containing:
   - typed signature with consent
   - attendee role
   - internal discussion notes
   - decision
   - task
   - organization contact data
   - attachment location
   - policy note
2. Save the record.
3. Click Partner Export on the saved-record card.
4. Choose Partner Safe.
5. Preview the package.

Confirm the copy includes operational content but does not include:

- typed signature
- signature consent
- signature verification
- signed timestamp
- internal discussion notes
- contact details
- attachment file location
- attachment added-by value
- policy note
- directory snapshot
- adapter or synchronization metadata

6. Download JSON.
7. Download HTML.
8. Confirm both communicate that they are redacted external copies.
9. Confirm the source record remains unchanged.

## Public Summary Export

1. Select Public Summary.
2. Preview a completed record.
3. Confirm the copy contains only high-level metadata, organizations, completed agenda items, approved or confirmed decisions, and summary.
4. Confirm attendee details, location, facilitator, tasks, attachments, notes, governance, and retention details are absent.

## Custom External Copy

1. Select Custom External Copy.
2. Test every section checkbox separately.
3. Include Discussion Notes and confirm a manual-review warning appears in the manifest.
4. Include attendees and confirm only name, organization / role, and attendance type remain.
5. Include attachments and confirm only name, type, and date remain.
6. Include retention and confirm only policy ID, review date, lifecycle status, and legal-hold active state remain.
7. Confirm signatures and verification fields never appear regardless of checkbox settings.

## Governance Export Gate

1. Save a Restricted Record.
2. Switch to a role that cannot export under the selected policy.
3. Select that record in Partner-Safe Export.
4. Preview the package.

Expected result: export is blocked with a clear message.

5. Switch to an allowed role.
6. Confirm preview and download work.

## Integrity Digest

On HTTPS or localhost:

1. Preview an external package.
2. Confirm the integrity algorithm is SHA-256.
3. Download JSON.
4. Confirm the package includes digest, canonicalization method, and disclaimer.

In an environment without Web Crypto:

1. Preview an external package.
2. Confirm the algorithm is explicitly labeled FNV-1a-32 compatibility checksum.
3. Confirm the note says it is non-cryptographic.

In every environment, confirm the UI never describes the digest as a digital signature or proof of identity.

## External Export Activity Log

1. Preview without downloading.
2. Export the activity log.
3. Confirm no entry was added for preview only.
4. Download JSON and HTML copies.
5. Export the activity log again.
6. Confirm one entry exists for each completed download.
7. Confirm log entries contain metadata and digest only, not meeting content.

## Migration

1. Seed a valid v1.0 active record, archived record, revision snapshot, and draft in localStorage.
2. Reload the app.
3. Confirm all four locations migrate to schema 1.1.0.
4. Confirm existing fields remain unchanged.
5. Confirm retention and redaction defaults are added.
6. Reload again.
7. Confirm no duplicate hold history or destructive rewrite occurs.
8. Inspect `methodzMigrationState` for successful completion.

## Archive Detail and Print

1. Open an active record Archive Page.
2. Confirm Retention & Preservation Status appears.
3. Confirm policy, review date, lifecycle status, and note are readable.
4. Open a record with active hold.
5. Confirm the visible hold warning appears.
6. Print or save PDF.
7. Confirm the retention section is included and interactive workspace panels are excluded.

## Mobile and Accessibility

At a narrow viewport:

- retention controls stack without horizontal scrolling
- badges wrap cleanly
- dashboard rows remain readable
- Partner-Safe Export controls remain touch friendly
- preview JSON scrolls inside its container

Using keyboard only:

- every v1.1 control is reachable
- labels announce meaningful names
- select controls and checkboxes operate normally
- focus remains visible
- disabled permanent deletion is announced as unavailable

## Failure Conditions

Do not mark v1.1 stable if any of the following occurs:

- the direct-file workflow fails
- source records change during redaction
- any typed signature appears in an external package
- an active legal hold record can be permanently deleted
- migrations remove unknown fields
- retention metadata disappears after edit or restore
- role restrictions are bypassed
- the service worker caches record data
- the UI calls a checksum a digital signature
